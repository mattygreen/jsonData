Arts

Except for one brief mention of Rastafari, the news story that dominates this collection is about the Notting Hill Carnival. This is in no way representative of the breadth and depth of the history of Black artists and cultures in the UK, but it is rather revealing of the ways in which the BBC, alongside other mainstream media and the press, shaped a racialised discourse around the carnival. The news scripts in this collection, ranging from the 1970s to the early 1990s, show that Radio 4’s coverage has consistently focused not on the cultural and creative elements and history of the carnival, but on incidents of crime, unrest and the role of the police. You can also hear our podcast contributors discussing the BBC’s coverage and reflecting on the historical role of the carnival, as well as their own memories and experiences of it.
