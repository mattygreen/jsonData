Sports

The news item that features most prominently in this collection is about the Stop the Seventy Tour campaign, organised by the Anti-Apartheid Movement (AAM) and its broader Campaign against Racial Discrimination. Stop the Seventy Tour led to the AAM’s first big victory – the cancellation of the all-white Springbok cricket series in June 1970. Other news stories here are about the careers and achievements of individual athletes, such as Dick Turpin, Anita Neil, Tessa Sanderson and John Conteh. This is by no means a comprehensive list of Black British athletes in the postwar years but it is, in some ways, indicative of what BBC radio news chose to report on. Key questions that emerged when we were researching these news stories are whether these athletes were identified racially, first and foremost; what kind of priority (if any) in sports news reporting they were given, but also the complete lack of reporting in some cases.
