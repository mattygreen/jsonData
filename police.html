Relations with Police

This collection covers news stories of police involvement, and/or handling of the deaths of Kelso Cochrane (1959),David Oluwale (1971) , Cynthia Jarrett (1985) , as well as coverage of the ‘Spaghetti House siege’ (1975), the sus law, and the inquest into the murder of Stephen Lawrence. Some of these cases and names are better known than others but they are all equally important for understanding media narratives that associated Black lives with illegality and violence. In their seminal book Policing the Crisis: Mugging, the State and Law and Order, originally published in 1978, Stuart Hall, Charles Critcher, Tony Jefferson, John Clarke and Brian Roberts offered an incisive analysis of how a certain ‘mugging’ incident in 1972 sparked off a massive press coverage that condensed themes of race, crime and youth into the image of ‘mugging’. The term was presented by the media as a new strain of crime, but more fundamentally it came to serve as a moral panic and an ideological construct that racialised and objectified Black youth and communities. Two news scripts in this collection relate to this racialised labelling. Finally, absences in news reporting speak as loudly as cases that are over-reported and Radio 4’s lack of reporting on what is known as the “Mangrove Nine” trial is a case in point.
